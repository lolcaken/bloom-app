Project Overview

The "Bloom Watch App" helps communities, researchers, and policymakers monitor and respond to harmful algal blooms (HABs). Using NASA satellite data and local reporting, it provides real-time information about water quality and bloom spread.

The app empowers people to track blooms, receive alerts, and make informed public health and environmental decisions. It's designed to be accessible to both experts and the general public.

Users can contribute to citizen science by reporting bloom activity and supporting a global monitoring network.

The Challenge

Create a mobile-friendly solution that improves our ability to monitor and respond to harmful algal blooms, combining NASA Earth data with user-friendly tools to track and predict bloom activity.

Key Features

  • Real-Time Monitoring: Detect blooms as they occur using NASA satellite data.
  • Alerts & Notifications: Send warnings to communities near affected water bodies.
  • Citizen Science: Users can report bloom sightings and water quality observations.
  • Data Visualization: Interactive maps and charts for researchers and public.
  • Educational Tools: Info about bloom causes, risks, and prevention strategies.

Key Considerations

  • Make satellite data easy to understand.
  • Support both local communities and researchers.
  • Improve tracking accuracy through citizen input.
  • Innovative compared to existing tools.
