Bloom Watch App

NASA Space Apps Challenge 2025

Moath AND Hafsa 8B

Project Overview

The "Bloom Watch App" is designed to help communities, researchers, and policymakers monitor and respond to harmful algal blooms (HABs). By using NASA satellite data and local reporting, the app provides real-time information about water quality and bloom spread.

This tool empowers people to track blooms, get alerts for affected areas, and support public health and environmental safety decisions. The app makes critical information accessible and actionable for both experts and the general public.

With Bloom Watch, users can stay informed and contribute to citizen science efforts, reporting bloom activity and helping build a global monitoring network.

```

The Challenge

 

Create a mobile-friendly solution that improves our ability to monitor and respond to harmful algal blooms. The app should combine NASA Earth data with user-friendly tools to track and predict bloom activity.

Key Features

  • Real-Time Monitoring: Use NASA satellite data to detect blooms as they occur.
  • Alerts & Notifications: Send warnings to communities near affected water bodies.
  • Citizen Science: Enable users to report bloom sightings and water quality observations.
  • Data Visualization: Interactive maps and charts for researchers and the public.
  • Educational Tools: Provide information about bloom causes, risks, and prevention strategies.

Key Considerations

  • How does the app make satellite data easy to understand?
  • How will it support both local communities and researchers?
  • How can citizen input improve bloom tracking accuracy?
  • What makes Bloom Watch innovative compared to existing tools?
```

Resources

 

NASA Data Sources

  • MODIS & VIIRS: Satellite imagery for detecting water quality changes.
  • Earth Observing System Data: Access to NASA’s Earth science datasets.
  • Global Precipitation Measurement: Rainfall data affecting bloom development.
  • NASA Earthdata Portal: Central hub for downloading Earth science data.

Tools and Technologies

  • Google Earth Engine: For satellite data analysis.
  • Leaflet.js: For interactive map visualizations.
  • TensorFlow: For predictive modeling of bloom activity.
  • React Native: For cross-platform mobile app development.

Educational Resources

  • NASA Earth Science: Information about oceans, lakes, and ecosystems.
  • NASA Applied Sciences: Case studies on harmful algal blooms.
  • Citizen Science Projects: Examples of community monitoring efforts.
